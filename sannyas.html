01  —  What is Sannyas

Not religion.
Not escape.
A way of living
with awareness.

Sannyas is a Sanskrit word meaning to let go — to renounce not the world, but the noise inside it.

"The one who has learned to observe without reacting has learned the most difficult thing."

This is not philosophy for the mind. It is practice for the body, the breath, the moment. What you do here, you carry nowhere — and that is the point.

Not this

Not a retreat from life.
Not a temporary fix.
Not spiritual tourism.

But this

A confrontation with what is.
A return to stillness.
A meeting with yourself.

The practice

Breath. Body. Observation.
Silence. Repetition. Honesty.

02  —  Why silence

The mind does not stop.
But you can learn
to watch it.

Inspired by Vipassana — one of the oldest meditation techniques — our retreats use silence as the primary instrument. Not as punishment. Not as restriction. As a mirror.

01

The body knows first

Sensation precedes thought. Every emotion lives in the body before the mind names it. Learning to observe sensation — without reaction — is the work.

02

Breath as anchor

The breath is always here. It does not belong to the past or future. Returning to it — thousands of times — trains the deepest part of attention.

03

Why 10 days

Change at this depth takes time. The first three days, the mind resists. By day five, something begins to soften. Day ten, you see what was always there.

04  —  The Experience

Simple.
Disciplined.
Unchanged

The environment is nature. The food is simple. The company is silence. Nothing is designed to be comfortable. Everything is designed to be clear.

05  —  Sessions & Urban Work

Not ready for
ten days?
Start here.

BLNDFLD is where Sannyas meets the city. Shorter, sharper, designed to crack open the ordinary enough to ask a real question.

◯

Blindfold Sessions

90 minutes. Eyes covered. Attention turned entirely inward. The removal of sight makes everything else louder — and then quieter.

∷

Philosophy Sessions

Not lectures. Inquiry. Questions like: What is suffering? What is awareness? What is the self? Held in small groups. No answers given.

◻

Urban Pop-Ups

Short-format sessions brought into cities. One hour of silence inside the loudest environments. An introduction to what practice can be.
